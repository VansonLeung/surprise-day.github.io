This Guy's Clapback Earned Him Applause At The Airport

Stories Feb 07 • 4 min read

It’s unacceptable that there is still racism in the world today. People who think they are superior to others just because they have a different skin color. This woman also made a racist comment to a man, but his answer was priceless!

For many, these situations may go unnoticed. But for those who suffer racism, they’re present everyday. Fortunately, social media help counter these disrespect for others, and racist people are publicly condemned for their attitude.

Did you hear the one about the African American guy standing in a priority boarding line at the airport who got told to get out of the way by a blonde lady who assumed he a) either got in the wrong line, and hence was standing in the way of rich people who deserve to be there, or b) must be there because he’s military and should still get out of the way because, again, he was in the way of people who actually paid to be there..?

No? Sorry, this one doesn’t have a punchline. It’s just another story of a viral post that turned the internet into a torch-bearing mob.

Very recently, a man by the name of Emmanuel Perry, a music executive in the United States, denounced a situation he suffered in his own skin. His story went viral on Facebook after alleging that he had experienced an instance of racial prejudice in Washington Airport.

In a post to Facebook that has since gone viral, Emmanuel Perry alleged a woman in Washington Airport assumed he was not a first class passenger: “Excuse me, I believe you may be in the wrong place you need to let us thru (sic.) This line is for priority boarding,” the woman said.

She reportedly refused to believe him and assumed he had to be a member of the military to be able to enjoy first class

Why would she even care how would someone pay for their ticket. She could have mind her own business, but she was way too rude to do that.

He posted the conversation with a selfie, showing him waiting in line with the woman standing behind him. It had been liked over 670,000 times at the time of writing, with hundreds of comments praising Perry.

His reply was not only supported by the passengers who were waiting to board, but by thousands of people who later liked and shared the post.
